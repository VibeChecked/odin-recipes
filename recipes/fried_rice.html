Egg Fried Rice Recipe

Egg Fried Rice

Here are the 8 ingredients needed

  • White rice, cooked and chilled
  • Eggs, lightly beaten
  • Scallions & garlic, finely chopped
  • Light soy sauce
  • Sesame oil
  • Salt & white pepper
  • Light soy sauce
  • Neutral cooking oil (olive oil isn’t suitable)

Preparation Phase (Rice)

Long grain or short grain rice is fine, although jasmine rice is the best choice (Most restaurants use it for fried rice). It creates a fluffier texture than the short-grain variety which is more starchy

  1. Day leftover rice is best. When it comes to plain steamed rice, it is preferred to be soft and a little sticky. However, intending to fry it the next day, make it on the dry side.
  2. Wait till cooked rice is completely cooled, then store it in the fridge until you’re ready to fry. Refrigeration helps to dehydrate and firm up the grains.
  3. Just before frying, loosen the grains as much as possible

Cooking Phase (Rice)

  1. First the eggs. Lightly beat the eggs until the white and yolk are well incorporated. Scramble the eggs into small pieces. Dish out to set aside
  2. Next, the rice. Add a little oil and fry minced garlic until it browns a little. Stir in the rice. Flip and toss constantly to heat the rice evenly. If there are still some clumps, press with a spatula to separate the rice grains.
  3. Combine and Season. After rice is hot, put in the scrambled egg and scallions. Add soy sauce, sesame oil, salt and white pepper. Give everything a good stir to evenly distribute the seasonings.

Note: It’s perfectly fine to use a skillet/frying pan. If non-stick, make sure not to heat it up empty.
